Substitute Colors in Images, like a Wizard.

What can take hours in Photoshop, finished in seconds.
          Last year one of my colleagues was given a task for recoloring b/w icons for all 7 colors of the rainbow. The task was not complicated but too monotonous. The only approach he knew was opening up each icon in Gimp, filling the black with a color using the fill tool, and saving it as a PNG. This would not have been such a big deal but the icons were just too many. It was 60 icons with each of them getting recolored 7 times i.e., 420 iterations of recoloring (holy shit). The guy was about to reach Chinese sweatshop levels of monotony with this task, but I didn't let it happen. I took over the task before he could begin (with his permission) by telling him that I have a method that can finish the job within minutes. Needless to say, he was more than happy to hand it over to me and was more than surprised to find that I have created all the required images in less than 10 minutes.
How did I do it? Magic. ImageMagick to be more specific.

In short, ImageMagick is a command-line tool for editing images; but this description does not do justice to it's wide range of capabilities. It is a literal supertool for editing images from the terminal. As the editing is done programmatically, there is no chance of errors that you'd normally make when editing an image by hand.

Here I've got 10 b/w PNG icons that will be our subjects for the recoloring process. These icons only contain one color: Black, the background is transparent. We want to recolor them with: Violet, Indigo, Blue, Green, Yellow, Orange and Red. That should give us 70 new images, 80 in total.

To substitute colors in ImageMagick we use the following command...
this example is for Red.

``` convert input.png -fill red -colorize 100 output.png ```

This will preserve the transparent pixels and make everything else red. With a simple nested loop in Powershell, I get everything done in one fell swoop.

And just like that, within minutes we have our 70 recolored images!
